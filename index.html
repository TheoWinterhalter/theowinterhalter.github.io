My name is Théo Winterhalter and I am a postdoctoral researcher at Max Planck Institute for Security and Privacy (MPI-SP) in Bochum (Germany). My main interest lies in type theory and its applications to proof assistants. As such, most of my results are formalised in a proof assistant, generally Coq. I strive to make proof assistants better and safer.

Big projects I have been working on

SSProve is a framework combining high-level modular proofs about composed cryptography protocols with a probabilistic relational program logic to treat lower-level details. All of it is formalised in Coq.

As a stress-test, we formalised in SSProve a security theorem for KEM-DEM. This proof follows the original SSP paper by Brzuska et al. Thanks to our formalisation effort, and in collaboration with the authors, we found a bug in their original theorem. This has led them to update their proof, which we then formalised in full in SSProve.

MetaCoq provides meta-programming facilities to the Coq proof assistant. MetaCoq contains a formal specification of Coq, including its meta-theory. We then prove most of the meta-theoretic results expected of it, and, assuming strong normalisation, we derive a type-checker for Coq, verified in Coq.

My Ph.D thesis

Formalisation and meta-theory of type theory

I was a PhD student of Nicolas Tabareau and Matthieu Sozeau within the Inria team Gallinette at LS2N in Nantes (France). In this thesis, I talk about the meta-theory of type theory and about how to formalise it in a proof assistant. I have worked on several topics during my PhD. I first worked on the relation between extensional type theory and intensional type theory. This work is then extended to weak type theory. Another big part of my thesis is about verifying a type-checker for Coq, in Coq itself. Finally, although it is not part of my manuscript, I have also worked on safely extending the theory of Coq or Agda with user-defined rewrite rules.
